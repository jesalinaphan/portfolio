Who is Vilde?

In some universe, Vilde is an elementary school teacher from Colorado. Her many years of teaching have made her a little bit inflexible, so her chair must help compensate for that. This ENGN0032 project involves making a chair for a specific persona to highlight the importance of user-based design.

With Vilde in mind, I brainstormed some important criteria that the chair should have:
- Goal: create a chair that increases comfort for our user when they sit on the ground to teach young children
- No higher than 3 in. off the ground so that she can sit and read to her students
- Ability to rotate while in chair, not overly large, and easily cleaned
- Vilde is also not super flexible, so the chair should not require flexibility to sit in
- Add to a happy, kid-friendly classroom aesthetic (a fun design, fidgets for kids, etc.)

Initial Prototypes + Final Design

After brainstorming, I created some quick low fidelity prototypes and decided on the elements of each design that I would want to include in the final version of the chair.

CAD Modeling + Testing

One of the most important aspects of a designing a chair is making sure that it is able to hold a substantial amount of weight. To check the weight distribution, I created a CAD model of the chair and used Fusion360's simulation software to test a force of 650N in the vertical direction and 50lbs in the horizontal direction.

Final Prototype

Using materials in the Brown Design Workshop, I thought up a simple Bill of Materials first and then created the final 1/4 scale prototype of the chair. Although the material used on the chair had a fun pattern, I could have used a material that was more easily cleanable, but other than that, pretty much all of the criteria I wanted to hit were accomplished!
